ABOUT THE DAY ABOUT THE DAY

Reframing Learning Spaces

Thursday 23 May 2019

This year we are having a learning and teaching festival, concerned with learning spaces, places, environments and sites.

The concept, use, and experience of 'space' in higher education deeply influences students and teachers. The festival will explore space as a physical or virtual ‘learning place’, and a ‘belonging space,’ in which learners can engage individually and collectively. How can we develop innovative ways of using space for learning across disciplines?

A large part of the day will concern ‘outdoor learning’, with a menu of ‘learning walks’ to choose from.  The walks could be circular, or be to a destination such as the library, a laboratory, or other learning space.

There will also be parallel talks in Julian Study Centre.

GET INVOLVED GET INVOLVED

We are looking for submissions of a maximum of 150 words around the stated theme. We are especially interested in practical ideas and potential solutions for the delivery of learning within and outside universities, nationally or internationally.  Talks should not exceed 20 minutes including questions, and walks should be one hour.  Submissions for either should answer the question ‘what will others learn from my session?’

Potential discussion areas include:

  • Psychological and social spaces
  • Dialogic and relational spaces
  • Digital learning
  • Designing infrastructure for technology-based learning
  • Laboratory learning
  • Off-campus learning
  • The outdoor learning environment

Displays of relevant material (accompanied by short demonstrations) may be set up in the TPSC foyer.

The deadline for submissions is 31 December 2018.
